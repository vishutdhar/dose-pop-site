Dose Pop

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026

The short version: Dose Pop is private by default. Your medicines stay on your device and in your own iCloud, and we never see them. Nothing leaves your device unless you turn on Caregiver Alerts and choose one person to share with. There are no ads and no tracking.

What we collect

By default, nothing. Dose Pop does not collect personal data and does not use advertising or tracking. The one exception is Caregiver Alerts, described below, which sends a limited amount of information only after you turn it on and pick who receives it.

Data stored on your device

Dose Pop stores your medication list, dosages, dose history, notification preferences, and app settings locally on your device using Apple frameworks.

iCloud sync

If iCloud sync is enabled in your Apple system settings, app data may sync through your own iCloud account. The developer does not have access to your iCloud data.

Caregiver Alerts (only if you turn it on)

Caregiver Alerts is off unless you choose to turn it on. When you do, you name one caregiver and give your express agreement on a consent screen before anything is shared. You can stop sharing at any time from within the app.

What is shared: the names of your medicines, their colors and scheduled times, and whether each dose was taken, skipped, or missed. Your dosages, instructions, and notes are never shared. The app removes them before anything is sent, and there is no place to store them on our side.

How your caregiver sees it: your caregiver receives an email with a private link to a simple web page that shows the shared information. They receive a further email only if a dose looks missed, after the grace period you set. They cannot see the app, cannot change anything, and never receive your dosages.

Where the shared data lives: the shared information is held by Cloudflare (Cloudflare Workers and the D1 database) so the link and the alerts can work, and caregiver emails are sent through Resend. These providers act only as processors for this feature. We do not sell, rent, or use your information for advertising, and we do not share it with anyone other than the caregiver you name.

Turning it off: when you remove your caregiver, the private link stops working right away. We keep a record that you had consented and when you revoked it, because that record is what makes the sharing an authorized, patient directed disclosure. Shared medicine data is removed after a period of inactivity.

Security: links are unguessable and expire, the shared page reveals nothing for an unknown or revoked link, and we keep a breach notification procedure ready. If we ever suspected that shared information was exposed, we would act on it and notify affected people as required.

For California residents

Dose Pop treats your medication and adherence information as confidential medical information under the California Confidentiality of Medical Information Act (CMIA). We disclose it only at your direction, to the caregiver you name, and only after your express agreement, and we honor your revocation. We do not disclose your medical information for any other purpose.

Medication search

When you search for a medication name, Dose Pop may make anonymous, read only requests to public medication databases such as RxNorm and OpenFDA. These requests contain the search text you type and do not include an account, advertising identifier, or tracking identifier.

Notifications

Dose Pop uses Apple local notifications to remind you when it is time to take a medication. Your reminders are scheduled and shown by your own device and do not pass through a Dose Pop server. Caregiver alert emails are separate and are covered above.

Children

Dose Pop is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from any user.

Medical disclaimer

Dose Pop is a reminder tool. It does not diagnose, treat, prescribe, or provide medical advice. Always follow instructions from your doctor, pharmacist, or other qualified healthcare professional.

Changes to this policy

If we change how Caregiver Alerts or any other feature handles your information, we will update this policy and the App Store privacy answers before the change takes effect.
